# 50 Japanese (Kampo & Folk) Herbal Remedies

> Sourcing: **Tsumura** and **Kracie** sell standardized Kampo granule formulas at pharmacies (ドラッグストア) — Matsumoto Kiyoshi, Welcia, Tsuruha. Single herbs at **Kampo-yaku-kyoku (漢方薬局)** specialty shops. Folk herbs and teas at supermarkets, depachika, and farmer’s markets (michi-no-eki).

## 1. Yomogi 蓬 (Japanese mugwort, *Artemisia princeps*)
- **Benefit:** Digestion, women’s health, skin, moxa.
- **Recipe:** Infusion: 1 tsp (≈2 g) dried leaf in 240 ml hot water, 8–10 min; 1–2 cups/day. Fresh young leaves pounded into *yomogi-mochi* as a seasonal food (spring).
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets (spring), moxibustion shops.

## 2. Shiso 紫蘇 (*Perilla frutescens*)
- **Benefit:** Allergies, antibacterial, cold.
- **Recipe:** Fresh leaves in tea, sashimi; red shiso in umeboshi.
- **Sourcing:** Every supermarket.

## 3. Dokudami ドクダミ (*Houttuynia cordata*)
- **Benefit:** "Ten medicines" — detox, skin, sinus.
- **Recipe:** Dried leaves roasted, infused as tea.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies (dokudami-cha).

## 4. Biwa-ha 枇杷葉 (loquat leaf)
- **Benefit:** Cough, skin, biwa compress therapy.
- **Recipe:** Decoction of 2–3 leaves; or leaf moxa compress.
- **Sourcing:** Kampo shops.

## 5. Ryokucha 緑茶 (green tea, *Camellia sinensis*)
- **Benefit:** Antioxidant, metabolism, oral health.
- **Recipe:** Sencha 70–80 °C, 1 min; matcha whisked.
- **Sourcing:** Everywhere; Uji, Shizuoka premium.

## 6. Shōga 生姜 (ginger)
- **Benefit:** Warming, cold, nausea.
- **Recipe:** *Shōga-yu*: 1 tsp freshly grated ginger + 1 tsp honey in 200 ml hot water; drink warm 1–3× daily for chills, early cold, nausea.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets; Kochi premium.

## 7. Umeboshi 梅干し (pickled plum)
- **Benefit:** Digestion, hangover, fatigue.
- **Recipe:** *Ume-shō-bancha*: 1 umeboshi (mashed) + 1 tsp shoyu + ½ tsp grated ginger + 200 ml hot bancha tea; stir, drink warm. Classic for cold, fatigue, hangover.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets; Wakayama best.

## 8. Yomogi-mochi 蓬餅
- **Benefit:** Seasonal digestive support.
- **Recipe:** 100 g sweet-rice flour (mochi) + 2 tbsp yomogi paste + water to a soft dough; steamed 20 min, shaped into cakes. Seasonal (hanami).
- **Sourcing:** Wagashi shops (spring).

## 9. Kuromoji 黒文字 (*Lindera umbellata*)
- **Benefit:** Aromatic digestive, anti-viral studies.
- **Recipe:** 10 g dried kuromoji twigs in 500 ml water, simmered 5–8 min; 1 cup warm, 1–2× daily. Aromatic after-meal digestive.
- **Sourcing:** Specialty tea shops; michi-no-eki.

## 10. Mitsuba 三つ葉 (*Cryptotaenia japonica*)
- **Benefit:** Aromatic, digestive, mild sedative.
- **Recipe:** 1 small handful fresh mitsuba leaves (≈5 g) added at the end of clear soups or chawanmushi, per serving. Culinary aromatic herb.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 11. Kuzu 葛 (*Pueraria lobata* root starch)
- **Benefit:** Diarrhea, cold, hangover; Kakkon-tō base.
- **Recipe:** *Kuzu-yu* — 1 tsp kuzu in cold water, then heat with ginger + shoyu.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets; Yoshino Nara premium.

## 12. Kakkon-tō 葛根湯 (classical formula)
- **Benefit:** Early cold with stiff neck/shoulders.
- **Recipe:** Tsumura No. 1: 1 sachet (2.5 g) in 100 ml hot water, 2–3× daily before meals. Begin at first sign of cold.
- **Sourcing:** Any pharmacy.

## 13. Shōsaikotō 小柴胡湯
- **Benefit:** Lingering cold, liver, digestion.
- **Recipe:** Tsumura No. 9: 1 sachet (2.5 g) in 100 ml hot water, 2–3× daily before meals. Prescription in Japan; avoid with interferon and chronic hepatitis without medical supervision.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies; Tsumura.

## 14. Hochu-ekki-tō 補中益気湯
- **Benefit:** Fatigue, weak digestion, post-illness.
- **Recipe:** Granules 2–3×/day.
- **Sourcing:** Tsumura, Kracie.

## 15. Kakkon-tō-ka-senkyū-shin’i 葛根湯加川芎辛夷
- **Benefit:** Sinus congestion.
- **Recipe:** Tsumura No. 2: 1 sachet (2.5 g) in warm water, 2–3× daily before meals for sinus-type colds.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies.

## 16. Bakumondō-tō 麦門冬湯
- **Benefit:** Dry cough, throat irritation.
- **Recipe:** Tsumura No. 29: 1 sachet (2.5 g) in warm water, 2–3× daily before meals for dry persistent cough.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies.

## 17. Daikenchūtō 大建中湯
- **Benefit:** Cold abdomen, post-op ileus.
- **Recipe:** Tsumura No. 100: 1 sachet (2.5 g) in warm water 2–3× daily before meals; widely used peri-operatively for post-op ileus and cold-type abdominal pain.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies.

## 18. Tōki-shakuyaku-san 当帰芍薬散
- **Benefit:** Menstrual pain, cold constitution in women.
- **Recipe:** Tsumura No. 23: 1 sachet (2.5 g) in hot water 2–3× daily before meals for menstrual pain, cold constitution in women.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies.

## 19. Kamishoyōsan 加味逗遥散
- **Benefit:** PMS, irritability, hot flashes.
- **Recipe:** Tsumura No. 24: 1 sachet (2.5 g) in hot water 2–3× daily before meals for PMS, menopausal irritability, hot flashes.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies.

## 20. Yokukansan 抑胝散
- **Benefit:** Irritability, insomnia, behavioral symptoms in dementia.
- **Recipe:** Tsumura No. 54: 1 sachet (2.5 g) in hot water 2–3× daily before meals for agitation, insomnia, BPSD in dementia.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies (doctor prescribed).

## 21. Rikkunshitō 六君子湯
- **Benefit:** Appetite, functional dyspepsia.
- **Recipe:** Tsumura No. 43: 1 sachet (2.5 g) in hot water 2–3× daily before meals for appetite loss and functional dyspepsia.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies.

## 22. Hangeshashintō 半夏瀉心湯
- **Benefit:** Nausea, diarrhea, chemo-induced mucositis.
- **Recipe:** Tsumura No. 14: 1 sachet (2.5 g) in warm water 2–3× daily before meals for chemo-induced mucositis and functional dyspepsia.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies.

## 23. Goshajinkigan 牛車腎気丸
- **Benefit:** Elderly lower-back weakness, chemo neuropathy.
- **Recipe:** Tsumura No. 107: 1 sachet (2.5 g) in warm water 2–3× daily before meals for lower-body cold, numbness, chemo-induced peripheral neuropathy.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies.

## 24. Mugi-cha 麦茶 (roasted barley tea)
- **Benefit:** Cooling, hydration, mild GI.
- **Recipe:** Cold-brew barley bag in 1 L water 2–4 hr.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 25. Sobacha 蕎麦茶 (buckwheat tea)
- **Benefit:** Circulation, rutin, blood pressure.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp roasted buckwheat in hot water.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 26. Genmaicha 玄米茶 (brown rice + green tea)
- **Benefit:** Digestion, warming tea.
- **Recipe:** 1 tbsp in 500 ml hot water.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 27. Hōjicha ほうじ茶 (roasted green tea)
- **Benefit:** Low caffeine, calming.
- **Recipe:** Steep 30 sec boiling water.
- **Sourcing:** Tea shops.

## 28. Matcha 抹茶
- **Benefit:** L-theanine + caffeine, focus, antioxidants.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp + 70 ml 80 °C water, whisk.
- **Sourcing:** Uji; tea shops.

## 29. Goma 胡麻 (sesame)
- **Benefit:** Calcium, lignans, skin.
- **Recipe:** 1 tbsp toasted black sesame over 1 bowl rice; or *gomashio*: 1 tbsp sesame + ½ tsp sea salt ground together, sprinkle 1–2 tsp per serving.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 30. Natto 納豆
- **Benefit:** Nattokinase, vitamin K2, gut.
- **Recipe:** 40–50 g (one standard pack) natto stirred with 1 tsp shoyu + ¼ tsp karashi mustard, served over 1 bowl of rice; 3–5× per week.
- **Sourcing:** All supermarkets.

## 31. Miso 味噌
- **Benefit:** Probiotic, isoflavones.
- **Recipe:** 1 tbsp miso in 200 ml dashi (not boiled).
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets; regional varieties.

## 32. Shoyu-kōji 醤油麹 / Shio-kōji 塩麹
- **Benefit:** Enzymes, digestion.
- **Recipe:** Marinade: 1 tbsp shoyu-kōji or shio-kōji per 100 g fish or vegetables; rest 30 min–2 hr before cooking.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 33. Konbu 昆布 (kelp)
- **Benefit:** Iodine, minerals, umami.
- **Recipe:** Cold-soak 1 hr for dashi.
- **Sourcing:** Hokkaido; supermarkets.

## 34. Wakame 若布
- **Benefit:** Minerals, fucoidan.
- **Recipe:** Miso soup: 1 tsp dried wakame (≈2 g) per 200 ml dashi, added at the end. Sunomono: 5 g dried wakame rehydrated 5 min with sliced cucumber + 1 tbsp rice vinegar + 1 tsp sugar.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 35. Hijiki ひじき
- **Benefit:** Iron, fiber (note arsenic — moderate use).
- **Recipe:** 10 g dried hijiki rehydrated 20 min, drained, simmered 15 min with 1 julienned carrot + 1 tbsp shoyu + 1 tsp mirin + 1 tsp sesame oil. Serves 2.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 36. Ume extract / Bainiku ekisu 梅肉エキス
- **Benefit:** Concentrated umeboshi paste, digestion, fatigue.
- **Recipe:** Pea-sized amount (≈½ tsp / ≈2 g) stirred in 100 ml warm water, 1–2× daily for acute diarrhea, food poisoning, digestive weakness.
- **Sourcing:** Health shops; Wakayama.

## 37. Ninniku 大蒜 (garlic) — Japanese folk
- **Benefit:** Cold, circulation.
- **Recipe:** Garlic-honey maceration 2 weeks.
- **Sourcing:** Aomori Fukuchi premium.

## 38. Aojiru 青汁 (green juice, kale/barley grass)
- **Benefit:** Greens supplement.
- **Recipe:** 1 sachet (3–4 g powder) in 150 ml cold water, juice, or milk; 1× daily.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 39. Amazake 甘酒
- **Benefit:** B-vitamins, digestion, "drinkable IV".
- **Recipe:** Rice + kōji fermented 8–10 hr at 55 °C.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 40. Yuzu 柚子
- **Benefit:** Cold prevention, circulation, *yuzu-yu* bath.
- **Recipe:** 3–5 whole yuzu fruits floated in a hot bath (*yuzu-yu*) on winter solstice (tōji). Peel scores optional. Skin reactions rare but possible.
- **Sourcing:** Kochi; supermarkets (winter).

## 41. Sanshō 山椒 (Japanese pepper)
- **Benefit:** Digestion, mild numbing.
- **Recipe:** Small pinch (≈¼ tsp) ground sanshō over grilled unagi or finished miso soup, per serving; or 1 tsp whole berries simmered in 1 L broth for aromatic heat.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 42. Wasabi 山葵 (*Eutrema japonicum*)
- **Benefit:** Antimicrobial, sinus clearing.
- **Recipe:** ½ tsp freshly grated wasabi root per 4 sashimi slices (≈50 g fish), paired with soy. Antimicrobial action is strongest in the first 20 min after grating.
- **Sourcing:** Shizuoka; real wasabi at specialty shops.

## 43. Kanten 寒天 (agar)
- **Benefit:** Fiber, satiety, cholesterol.
- **Recipe:** 1 g in 200 ml water, boiled; cold jelly.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 44. Gobō ごぼう (burdock root)
- **Benefit:** Fiber, inulin, skin.
- **Recipe:** *Kinpira gobō*: 1 medium burdock root (≈150 g) julienned, stir-fried with 1 tbsp shoyu + 1 tsp mirin + 1 tsp sesame oil + chili, 5–7 min. Serves 2.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 45. Renkon 蓮根 (lotus root)
- **Benefit:** Traditional cough, throat.
- **Recipe:** 2 tbsp freshly grated lotus-root juice + ¼ tsp grated ginger + 1 tsp honey in 100 ml warm water; drink 1–3× daily for sore throat and cough.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 46. Daikon 大根
- **Benefit:** Digestion, cough (daikon-ame).
- **Recipe:** ½ daikon radish (≈150 g) cubed, covered in honey 8–12 hr, strained. 1 tsp syrup 3–4× daily for cough; refrigerate, use within 2 weeks.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 47. Kinkan 金柑 (kumquat)
- **Benefit:** Sore throat, cough.
- **Recipe:** 10 kumquats halved (seeds removed) + 3 tbsp honey, rested 24 hr at room temperature. 1 tsp in 100 ml warm water, 2–3× daily for cough.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets (winter).

## 48. Karin 花梨 (Chinese quince)
- **Benefit:** Cough, throat.
- **Recipe:** Sliced in honey 2 weeks → syrup.
- **Sourcing:** Autumn markets.

## 49. Kikurage きくらげ (wood ear)
- **Benefit:** Circulation, blood.
- **Recipe:** 5 g dried wood-ear mushroom rehydrated 20 min in warm water, drained, stir-fried with vegetables. 1 serving 2–3×/week.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 50. Akamoku アカモク (seaweed)
- **Benefit:** Fucoidan, metabolism, thyroid support (caution with iodine).
- **Recipe:** 2 tbsp prepared akamoku seaweed with 1 tsp ponzu, served over 1 bowl rice; 1 serving 2–3×/week.
- **Sourcing:** Coastal supermarkets.
