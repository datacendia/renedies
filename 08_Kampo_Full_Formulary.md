# Kampo Full Formulary — Classical Japanese Herbal Formulas

Standardized Kampo formulas covered by Japanese health insurance are produced mainly by **Tsumura (ツムラ)** and **Kracie (クラシエ)**, numbered (Tsumura uses numbers 1–138+). Each entry: **Tsumura No. — Formula name (kanji / romaji) — primary use — typical dosage**.

> Dosage is usually one sachet (typically 2.5–3 g granule) 2–3× daily before meals in warm water, unless noted. Always follow package directions or doctor’s prescription.

## Folk home formulas (non-Tsumura)
- **Ume-shō-bancha** — umeboshi + shoyu + ginger + bancha; colds, fatigue, hangover.
- **Kuzu-yu** — kuzu + ginger + shoyu; diarrhea, colds, post-illness.
- **Daikon-ame** — daikon cubes in honey overnight; cough syrup.
- **Shoga-yu** — grated ginger + honey + hot water; wind-cold.
- **Yuzu-yu bath** — whole yuzu in bathwater on winter solstice.
- **Biwa compress** — dried loquat leaf + moxa over skin; joint pain, tumors (traditional).
- **Konnyaku compress** — hot boiled konnyaku wrapped, applied to liver/abdomen for warming.

## Notes on safety
- **Ephedra-containing formulas** (Nos. 27, 19, 127, 55, 95, 78, 28) — avoid in hypertension, heart disease, pregnancy, with MAOIs/stimulants.
- **Licorice (Kanzo)** is in many formulas — chronic combined use risks pseudoaldosteronism (low K, high BP, edema). Monitor if using multiple Kampo long-term.
- **Shosaikoto (No. 9)** — contraindicated with interferon; rare interstitial pneumonitis.
- **Daio (rhubarb)** laxative formulas — avoid long-term and in pregnancy.
- Kampo is prescribed by MDs in Japan under *sho* (pattern) diagnosis — pattern-matching improves results and reduces side effects.
