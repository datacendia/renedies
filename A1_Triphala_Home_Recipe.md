# Triphala — Detailed Home Preparation

**Triphala** (Skt. *tri* = three, *phala* = fruit) is the classical Ayurvedic blend of three dried fruits:

| Fruit | Sanskrit | Latin | Traditional action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Indian gooseberry | Amalaki | *Phyllanthus emblica* | Cooling, rasayana, vitamin C |
| Chebulic myrobalan | Haritaki | *Terminalia chebula* | Warming, mild laxative, "king of herbs" |
| Beleric myrobalan | Bibhitaki | *Terminalia bellirica* | Drying, respiratory, hair |

## Classical ratios
- **Equal parts (1 : 1 : 1)** — most common general formulation.
- **Seasonal variation** (some schools):
  - *Vata*: 2 haritaki : 1 amalaki : 1 bibhitaki (warming, cold season).
  - *Pitta*: 2 amalaki : 1 haritaki : 1 bibhitaki (cooling, hot season).
  - *Kapha*: 2 bibhitaki : 1 amalaki : 1 haritaki (drying, spring).

## Home preparation (powder / churna)

**Ingredients**
- 50 g dried amalaki fruit (seedless)
- 50 g dried haritaki fruit (seedless)
- 50 g dried bibhitaki fruit (seedless)

**Method**
1. Inspect fruits; ensure pits are removed and fruits are dry (no moisture → molds).
2. Dry-roast each separately on low heat 2–3 minutes until aromatic — not browned. This deactivates surface microbes.
3. Cool completely.
4. Grind in a spice grinder in small batches to fine powder.
5. Sift through a fine mesh; re-grind coarse bits.
6. Store in a clean, dry, airtight **amber glass jar** away from light and heat. Shelf life ~6 months.

## Dosage (adult)
- **General:** ½–1 tsp (2–4 g) once daily.
- **Best time:** 30–60 minutes after dinner, or at bedtime.
- **Vehicle (anupana):**
  - Warm water → general daily use.
  - Honey (added after warming, not boiled) → kapha, respiratory.
  - Ghee → vata, dryness, nervous system.
  - Decoction (overnight steep) → eye wash *triphala kashaya* (strained well, at body temperature, using sterile water).

## Decoction / Kashaya
1. 1 tbsp triphala powder in 2 cups water.
2. Steep overnight covered, OR simmer 10 min then strain.
3. Drink 100–150 ml warm in morning on empty stomach.

## Eye wash (traditional)
1. Prepare decoction above.
2. **Strain twice** through fine muslin, then through coffee filter.
3. Cool to body temp. Use in an eye cup, 30 seconds per eye.
4. **Only use freshly made, sterile decoction.** Discard after one use.

## Traditional benefits
- Gentle daily bowel regulation (not habit-forming).
- Gut-mucosa repair, microbiome balance.
- Eye health (internal + eyewash).
- Supports liver and cholesterol.
- Antioxidant / rasayana (rejuvenative) for long-term use.

## Cautions
- **Pregnancy:** avoid regular internal use — haritaki can be too strong.
- **Breastfeeding:** use only under practitioner guidance.
- **Diarrhea or dehydration:** discontinue.
- **Blood thinners, diabetes meds, thyroid meds:** consult doctor — may potentiate.
- **Children:** smaller dose (¼ tsp) only under guidance.
- **Allergy:** discontinue if rash / GI distress.

## Quality tips
- Buy organic, seedless, single-origin fruits if making at home.
- Commercial powders vary — look for: organic certification, no fillers, country of origin stated, ideally batch-tested for heavy metals (ash, lead, mercury).
- Trusted brands: **Banyan Botanicals**, **Organic India**, **Kottakkal**, **Baidyanath**, **Pukka**.
