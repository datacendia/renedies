# Ashwagandha (*Withania somnifera*) — Safety, Side Effects & Interactions

Ashwagandha is a well-studied adaptogen used for stress, sleep, strength, and fertility. While generally well-tolerated short-term, it has meaningful contraindications.

## Typical dosage (adult)
- **Root powder:** 1–6 g/day.
- **Standardized extract (e.g., KSM-66, Sensoril):** 300–600 mg/day, divided.
- **Decoction / milk decoction (kshira paka):** ½–1 tsp root powder simmered 10 min in milk, once daily.
- **Duration:** Most clinical trials run 8–12 weeks. Traditionally used longer, but periodic breaks (e.g., 6 weeks on / 2 off) are prudent.

## Common side effects (usually mild)
- Upset stomach, nausea, loose stools (especially on empty stomach — take with food).
- Drowsiness (usually desired; take at night if so).
- Headache in first few days.
- Increased body heat / "warming" feeling (pitta aggravation) — worse in hot climates or pitta constitution.

## Less common
- Skin rash / allergy.
- Mild liver enzyme elevations — **rare case reports of drug-induced liver injury (DILI)** exist; discontinue if dark urine, jaundice, nausea, fatigue appear.
- Worsening acid reflux.
- Vivid dreams.

## Contraindications — **avoid or use only with supervision**
- **Pregnancy:** traditionally abortifacient in large doses; avoid.
- **Breastfeeding:** insufficient safety data; avoid.
- **Autoimmune disease** (Hashimoto’s, Graves’, lupus, MS, RA): may stimulate immune activity — use only with specialist guidance.
- **Hyperthyroidism:** ashwagandha raises T3/T4 in many people; can worsen hyperthyroid symptoms. *Conversely it can help hypothyroid — but still monitor labs.*
- **Active liver disease:** avoid given DILI reports.
- **Prostate cancer (hormone-sensitive):** conflicting evidence; avoid without oncologist input.
- **Scheduled surgery:** stop ≥ 2 weeks before (sedative + possible bleeding effects).
- **Nightshade allergy:** Solanaceae family — use caution.
- **Children:** use only under practitioner guidance.

## Drug interactions
- **Sedatives** (benzodiazepines, Z-drugs, barbiturates, alcohol): additive CNS depression.
- **Thyroid medication** (levothyroxine): may increase thyroid hormones — monitor TSH/T4.
- **Immunosuppressants** (after transplant, methotrexate, biologics): may antagonize.
- **Diabetes medications:** may lower blood sugar further.
- **Antihypertensives:** may lower blood pressure further.
- **Hepatotoxic drugs** (acetaminophen high-dose, statins, antifungals): theoretical added liver stress.
- **MAOIs / SSRIs:** limited data; introduce cautiously.
- **Testosterone-modulating therapy:** ashwagandha may raise testosterone/DHEA-S.

## Quality & sourcing guidance
- Prefer **root** (not leaf) products — most classical use.
- Look for standardized extracts (KSM-66 or Sensoril) with a Certificate of Analysis for:
  - Withanolide content (2.5–5% for KSM-66).
  - Heavy metals (lead, mercury, arsenic, cadmium).
  - Pesticides and microbial limits.
- Trusted brands: **Himalaya**, **Organic India**, **Banyan Botanicals**, **Pukka**, **Ixoreal KSM-66**, **Jarrow**.
- Avoid unbranded bulk churna of unknown origin — Ayurvedic heavy-metal contamination has been documented in some uncertified products.

## Signs to stop and consult a doctor
- Jaundice, dark urine, right-upper-quadrant pain.
- Persistent nausea, vomiting, severe diarrhea.
- Palpitations, tremor, heat intolerance (possible thyroid over-stimulation).
- Rash, swelling, breathing difficulty (allergy).
- Worsening mood or sleep after 2+ weeks.

## Summary
Short-term (up to ~3 months), moderate-dose ashwagandha is well-tolerated for most healthy adults. It is **not** appropriate in pregnancy, uncontrolled hyperthyroidism, autoimmune flare, active liver disease, or alongside certain medications without medical supervision.
