Production Steps (Per Video)

Replace [KIT] with your market number. Do all 9 steps for Kit 1 (Global) first, then repeat for Kits 2–5.

Step 1 — Generate Soul ID Training Set

⏱ 30 min · Tool: Nano Banana Pro

Goal: 20–30 high-quality images of the same character to feed into Soul ID. Single-image training = "Bad" results. Volume + consistency = "Excellent".

  1. Sidebar → Image → Nano Banana Pro (or Ctrl+K → "Nano Banana")
  2. Open Soul ID Training Prompts and pick 8–10 prompts from your kit's set:
    • Kit 1 → T1.1 through T1.20
    • Kit 2 → T2.1 through T2.20
    • Kit 3 → T3.1 through T3.20
    • Kit 3.5 → T3.5.1 through T3.5.20
    • Kit 4 → T4.1 through T4.20
    • Kit 5 → T5.1 through T5.20
  3. For each prompt: Aspect 9:16, Quality High, Variations 4 → Generate
  4. Reject any image where face shape, eye color, hair texture, or age drifts. Keep only "clearly the same person" results.
  5. Target 20–30 keepers minimum. Save them all to [Market]/training/
Quality gate: Don't proceed to Step 2 until you have 20+ keepers that match the locked Character Bible for your kit. Garbage in = garbage Soul ID.

Universal — Motion Direction (All Talking Heads)

Two versions. Pick based on funnel stage:

Universal — Hook Variant For acquisition reels (default)
Engaged direct-to-camera presence, hands actively gesturing toward the prop, occasional brief sharp emphasis nod when delivering a key word, mouth animated naturally with speech, eyes locked to camera 80% of the time. Warm but alert — not sleepy. Slight forward lean of upper body. Energy reads as caring grandmother who has just realized you need to know this.
Universal — Calm Variant For retention / authority content
Subtle natural head movement, occasional warm smile, gentle blinks, hands stay holding the prop. Calm grandmotherly presence. Use this for longer-form educational content and PDF-explainer reels, NOT for 15-second viral hooks.
When to use which: Hook Variant for the first reel of a new hook (acquisition). Calm Variant for follow-up content where you're explaining the protocol in detail (retention). Same Soul ID character, different motion energy.

Realism Layer — Windows & Background Life

AI-generated images often feel staged because the background is empty. Adding a window with distant, soft-focus figures moving past creates depth and life without distracting from the herbalist. This is a real cinematography technique — "world-building depth." ✅ The Realism Layer is already baked into both the 6 hero images (full version) and all 120 Soul ID training prompts (concise version). The standalone snippets below are kept here as reference — use them if you want to add the same effect to a b-roll or tweak the wording.

⚠️ Rule: Background figures must be distant, blurred, and out of focus — never sharp, never named, never doing anything specific. Their job is to imply a living world, not compete with the subject. If they're too sharp the Soul ID character lock will degrade.
✅ Where the Realism Layer is applied:
• All 120 Soul ID training prompts — short concise version ("Distant blurred figures softly visible through the [window]"), so the trained character is aware of the brand aesthetic from day one
• All 6 hero images (Prompt X.1) — full descriptive version, baked in
• Optional on b-roll stills — grab the matching snippet below if you want background life in a still

If Soul ID character lock comes back weaker than expected, try regenerating a few training images without the realism phrase to isolate which is causing drift.
Realism — Kit 1 (Global)
Through a soft-focus modern window behind her, the faint blurred silhouettes of one or two pedestrians walking past on a sunny urban street, very distant and softly out of focus, adding a gentle sense of life and movement to the background. Window light remains the dominant illumination.
Realism — Kit 2 (India)
Through the carved wooden lattice (jali) window behind her, the faint blurred glimpse of one or two figures in saris walking slowly through a sunny courtyard far in the distance, very soft and out of focus, adding a quiet sense of village life. Golden lattice light remains the dominant illumination.
Realism — Kit 3 (Peru)
Through a small wooden window behind her, the faint blurred silhouettes of one or two figures in traditional Andean dress walking along a distant mountain path, very small in frame and softly out of focus, adding a sense of ancestral village life. Dawn light remains the dominant illumination.
Realism — Kit 3.5 (Brazil)
Through the open shuttered varanda window behind her, the faint blurred silhouettes of one or two figures slowly passing through a sun-drenched tropical garden far in the distance, banana leaves and philodendron framing the view, very soft and out of focus, adding a sense of Bahian quintal life. Tropical morning light remains the dominant illumination.
Realism — Kit 4 (China)
Through a translucent paper-screen window behind her, the faint moving silhouettes of one or two figures crossing a distant courtyard, almost abstract and very softly diffused through the paper, adding a sense of quiet apothecary life. Lantern glow remains the dominant illumination.
Realism — Kit 5 (Japan)
Through the shoji screen behind her, the gentle abstract silhouettes of one or two distant figures moving softly past, diffused almost to pure shape by the paper, very slow and contemplative, adding a sense of wabi-sabi rhythm. Shoji-screen daylight remains the dominant illumination.
💡 How to use: When you copy a kit's Prompt X.1 (herbalist image), paste the matching Realism snippet at the end of the prompt, before the final "vertical 9:16" line. The character bible stays locked; only the background gains life. If Soul ID consistency drops after adding the layer, simplify to just "distant blurred figure passing the window" — less is more.

E4 — Hook Burn-In (CapCut overlay, not auto-captions)

Apply to seconds 0.0 → 1.5 of every video

CapCut auto-captions misread accented languages — Hindi gets transliterated wrong, Portuguese drops the tilde, Mandarin tones collapse. The hook is the first 1.5 seconds, and if it lands wrong on screen you've already lost the viewer. Solution: burn the first 3–5 words in manually as a text overlay.

  1. In CapCut, after dropping in the Lipsync MP4, Text → Add Text at frame 0
  2. Type the first 3–5 words of the script in ALL CAPS (the spoken hook):
    • Kit 1 (EN): IF YOU WAKE UP
    • Kit 2 (HI): क्या आप थके हैं?
    • Kit 3 (ES): ¿SIENTES PESADEZ?
    • Kit 3.5 (PT): VOCÊ ACORDA CANSADA?
    • Kit 4 (ZH): 早上起来累吗？
    • Kit 5 (JA): 朝、疲れていませんか？
  3. Style: Noto Sans Bold · 110px · white text · 6px black stroke · 4px drop shadow · positioned top-third (avoid mouth area)
  4. Timing: In at 0.0s, out at 1.5s with a 0.2s fade-out
  5. Now proceed with Captions → Auto Captions for the remaining ~14s — the auto-captions should start their first segment at ~1.5s, not 0.0s. Drag their start point if needed.
Why not bake into the Nano Banana image? The herbalist still becomes a 16-second video via Lipsync — text baked into the source image stays for the full duration. We only want it for the first 1.5s. CapCut overlay = correct tool.
Localization gotcha: Hindi (Devanagari), Mandarin, and Japanese require Noto Sans Devanagari / CJK SC / CJK JP respectively. CapCut bundles them but the default font picker uses Latin Noto Sans which renders Devanagari as boxes. Always switch font per kit before typing.

Audio out of sync after cutsIn CapCut: select audio → "Snap to original position"
First 1.5s feels weakUse macro pour with motion-blur, add 0.5s zoom-burst
Video washed outReduce Vintage Warm filter to 40%
Music drowns voiceMusic -22dB, voice -3dB, enable ducking
