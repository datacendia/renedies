How each entry is structured

  • Preparation / Recipe — typical home preparation (decoction, infusion, powder, tincture, topical).
  • Local Sourcing — where the plant/product is typically bought in its country of origin.

Safety disclaimer

This is educational material, not medical advice. Herbs can interact with medications and have contraindications in pregnancy, breastfeeding, pediatric use, or with chronic disease. Always:

  1. Verify correct botanical identification (Latin binomial).
  2. Start with low doses, watch for allergy.
  3. Avoid long-term high-dose use without supervision.
  4. Check legality in your jurisdiction (e.g., coca leaf, ephedra, kava, kratom).
  5. Consult a licensed herbalist, Ayurvedic doctor, TCM practitioner, Kampo doctor, or MD.

Preparation glossary

  • Decoction — simmer hard plant parts (root, bark, berries) 15–30 min covered.
  • Infusion / Tea — pour hot water on leaves/flowers, steep 5–10 min covered.
  • Tincture — 1 part dried herb : 5 parts 40–60% alcohol, macerate 2–6 weeks, strain.
  • Poultice — crushed fresh herb applied to skin, covered with cloth.
  • Oil infusion — dried herb in olive/coconut oil, warm 2–4 hours double-boiler or 2–6 weeks sun.
  • Syrup — strong decoction + equal weight honey/sugar, refrigerate.

50 Indian / Ayurvedic Herbal Remedies

Sourcing notes: Pan-India Ayurvedic chains include Patanjali, Dabur, Himalaya, Baidyanath, Kottakkal Arya Vaidya Sala, Organic India. Online: Amazon.in, PharmEasy, 1mg, Netmeds. Traditional shops: local Panwadi, kirana, and Ayurveda Vaidya clinics.

1. Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)

  • Benefit: Adaptogen; reduces cortisol, improves sleep, strength, male fertility.
  • Recipe: ½–1 tsp root powder simmered 5 min in warm milk + ½ tsp ghee + honey; at night.
  • Sourcing: Ayurvedic pharmacies nationwide; cultivated in MP, Rajasthan, Gujarat.

2. Turmeric / Haldi (Curcuma longa)

  • Benefit: Anti-inflammatory, joint, liver, gut, wound healing.
  • Recipe: Golden milk — ½ tsp turmeric + pinch black pepper + 1 tsp ghee in warm milk.
  • Sourcing: Every grocery/spice shop; best from Erode (TN), Sangli (MH), Nizamabad.

3. Triphala (Amla + Haritaki + Bibhitaki)

  • Benefit: Gentle detox, regular bowels, eye health, gut lining.
  • Recipe: ½–1 tsp powder in warm water at bedtime, or steep overnight and drink morning.
  • Sourcing: Ayurvedic pharmacies; loose at herbal bazaars (Khari Baoli, Delhi).

4. Ginger / Adrak (Zingiber officinale)

  • Benefit: Nausea, digestion, cold/flu, circulation.
  • Recipe: Fresh ginger tea — 1-inch slice boiled 10 min, tulsi + honey + lemon.
  • Sourcing: Any sabzi mandi; best from Kerala, Meghalaya.

5. Amla / Indian Gooseberry (Phyllanthus emblica)

  • Benefit: Vitamin C, immunity, hair, liver, blood sugar.
  • Recipe: 30 ml fresh juice + water morning; or 1 tsp powder in water.
  • Sourcing: Seasonal (winter) fresh in markets; year-round powder/murabba in Ayurvedic shops.

6. Licorice / Mulethi (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

  • Benefit: Sore throat, cough, gastric ulcers, adrenal support.
  • Recipe: 1 small stick boiled 10 min in water; 1 cup up to 2× daily (≤ 6 weeks).
  • Sourcing: Spice shops, paan shops, Ayurvedic pharmacies.
  • Caution: Raises blood pressure; avoid in hypertension.

7. Brahmi (Bacopa monnieri)

  • Benefit: Memory, focus, anxiety, nervous system.
  • Recipe: ½ tsp powder in warm ghee/milk; or 5 ml juice of fresh plant.
  • Sourcing: Ayurvedic shops; grows wild in wetlands across India.

8. Cinnamon / Dalchini (Cinnamomum verum)

  • Benefit: Blood sugar, circulation, anti-microbial.
  • Recipe: ½ tsp powder in warm water + honey; or stick in chai.
  • Sourcing: Spice shops; prefer Ceylon variety from Kerala.

9. Moringa / Sahjan (Moringa oleifera)

  • Benefit: Nutrient-dense, blood sugar, cholesterol, anemia.
  • Recipe: 1 tsp leaf powder in water/soup; pods in sambar/dal.
  • Sourcing: Vegetable markets (fresh pods/leaves); powder in health stores.

10. Aloe vera / Ghritkumari (Aloe barbadensis)

  • Benefit: Skin healing, constipation, gastritis.
  • Recipe: 1 tbsp fresh gel + water or amla juice, empty stomach.
  • Sourcing: Home pots; gel bottles in supermarkets.

11. Tulsi / Holy Basil (Ocimum sanctum)

  • Benefit: Adaptogen, respiratory, fever, stress, blood sugar.
  • Recipe: 5–7 fresh leaves boiled with ginger + black pepper + cloves for kadha.
  • Sourcing: Grown in most Indian homes; dried tulsi teas widely sold.

12. Neem (Azadirachta indica)

  • Benefit: Skin diseases, blood purifier, antiparasitic, dental.
  • Recipe: 4–5 fresh leaves chewed morning; neem oil topical for acne/scabies.
  • Sourcing: Neem trees ubiquitous; oil and capsules at pharmacies.

13. Giloy / Guduchi (Tinospora cordifolia)

  • Benefit: Fever, immunity, chronic inflammation, liver.
  • Recipe: 1-inch stem crushed, boiled 10 min in water; 50 ml twice daily.
  • Sourcing: Climbing vine in home gardens; Ayurvedic shops sell stem/juice.

14. Shatavari (Asparagus racemosus)

  • Benefit: Female reproductive tonic, lactation, menopause.
  • Recipe: 1 tsp powder in warm milk + honey, at night.
  • Sourcing: Ayurvedic pharmacies; cultivated in MP/Maharashtra.

15. Fenugreek / Methi (Trigonella foenum-graecum)

  • Benefit: Blood sugar, cholesterol, lactation, digestion.
  • Recipe: 1 tsp seeds soaked overnight; drink water + chew seeds morning.
  • Sourcing: Every kirana store; fresh leaves in winter markets.

16. Cumin / Jeera (Cuminum cyminum)

  • Benefit: Digestion, bloating, iron.
  • Recipe: Jeera water — 1 tsp seeds boiled in 2 cups water 5 min, strain.
  • Sourcing: Spice shops nationwide.

17. Coriander / Dhania (Coriandrum sativum)

  • Benefit: Cooling, urinary health, digestion.
  • Recipe: 1 tsp seeds soaked overnight, drink water.
  • Sourcing: Any grocery; fresh leaves daily.

18. Cardamom / Elaichi (Elettaria cardamomum)

  • Benefit: Digestion, breath freshener, mild diuretic.
  • Recipe: 2 pods crushed in chai or warm water.
  • Sourcing: Spice shops; best from Kerala, Sikkim.

19. Black pepper / Kali Mirch (Piper nigrum)

  • Benefit: Bioavailability (piperine), digestion, cold.
  • Recipe: Pinch with turmeric + honey for cough; in trikatu formula.
  • Sourcing: Spice shops; Malabar pepper prized.

20. Long pepper / Pippali (Piper longum)

  • Benefit: Respiratory, kindling digestion, rasayana.
  • Recipe: ¼ tsp powder + honey 2× daily for cough.
  • Sourcing: Ayurvedic pharmacies.

21. Trikatu (Pippali + Pepper + Ginger)

  • Benefit: Digestive fire, metabolism, cold/kapha.
  • Recipe: ¼ tsp powder + honey before meals.
  • Sourcing: Ayurvedic pharmacies ready-made.

22. Bitter gourd / Karela (Momordica charantia)

  • Benefit: Blood sugar, liver, skin.
  • Recipe: 30 ml fresh juice + water, empty stomach.
  • Sourcing: Vegetable markets year-round.

23. Vasaka / Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda)

  • Benefit: Cough, bronchitis, asthma.
  • Recipe: 5 ml leaf juice + honey 2–3× daily; or decoction 30 ml.
  • Sourcing: Ayurvedic shops; grown as hedge.

24. Arjuna bark (Terminalia arjuna)

  • Benefit: Cardiac tonic, blood pressure, cholesterol.
  • Recipe: 1 tsp bark powder in milk + water (1:1), boiled down to milk-only.
  • Sourcing: Ayurvedic pharmacies (powder, tablets).

25. Gokshura (Tribulus terrestris)

  • Benefit: Urinary, kidney stones, male vigor.
  • Recipe: 1 tsp powder in warm water/milk, twice daily.
  • Sourcing: Ayurvedic shops.

26. Punarnava (Boerhavia diffusa)

  • Benefit: Diuretic, edema, liver, kidney.
  • Recipe: Decoction 50 ml twice daily; fresh greens in sabzi.
  • Sourcing: Weedy plant; Ayurvedic shops sell powder.

27. Kutki (Picrorhiza kurroa)

  • Benefit: Liver protective, bitter tonic, jaundice.
  • Recipe: ¼ tsp powder + honey 2× daily (bitter).
  • Sourcing: Himalayan herb; specialty Ayurvedic suppliers.

28. Bhringraj (Eclipta prostrata)

  • Benefit: Hair growth, liver, insomnia.
  • Recipe: Bhringraj oil massaged into scalp; or 1 tsp powder in water.
  • Sourcing: Weedy in wetlands; oils in all Ayurvedic shops.

29. Jatamansi (Nardostachys jatamansi)

  • Benefit: Anxiety, insomnia, hair, mind-calming.
  • Recipe: ¼–½ tsp powder in warm milk at night.

Triphala — Detailed Home Preparation

Triphala (Skt. tri = three, phala = fruit) is the classical Ayurvedic blend of three dried fruits:

Fruit Sanskrit Latin Traditional action
Indian gooseberry Amalaki Phyllanthus emblica Cooling, rasayana, vitamin C
Chebulic myrobalan Haritaki Terminalia chebula Warming, mild laxative, "king of herbs"
Beleric myrobalan Bibhitaki Terminalia bellirica Drying, respiratory, hair

Classical ratios

  • Equal parts (1 : 1 : 1) — most common general formulation.
  • Seasonal variation (some schools):
    • Vata: 2 haritaki : 1 amalaki : 1 bibhitaki (warming, cold season).
    • Pitta: 2 amalaki : 1 haritaki : 1 bibhitaki (cooling, hot season).
    • Kapha: 2 bibhitaki : 1 amalaki : 1 haritaki (drying, spring).

Home preparation (powder / churna)

Ingredients

  • 50 g dried amalaki fruit (seedless)
  • 50 g dried haritaki fruit (seedless)
  • 50 g dried bibhitaki fruit (seedless)

Method

  1. Inspect fruits; ensure pits are removed and fruits are dry (no moisture → molds).
  2. Dry-roast each separately on low heat 2–3 minutes until aromatic — not browned. This deactivates surface microbes.
  3. Cool completely.
  4. Grind in a spice grinder in small batches to fine powder.
  5. Sift through a fine mesh; re-grind coarse bits.
  6. Store in a clean, dry, airtight amber glass jar away from light and heat. Shelf life ~6 months.

Dosage (adult)

  • General: ½–1 tsp (2–4 g) once daily.
  • Best time: 30–60 minutes after dinner, or at bedtime.
  • Vehicle (anupana):
    • Warm water → general daily use.
    • Honey (added after warming, not boiled) → kapha, respiratory.
    • Ghee → vata, dryness, nervous system.
    • Decoction (overnight steep) → eye wash triphala kashaya (strained well, at body temperature, using sterile water).

Decoction / Kashaya

  1. 1 tbsp triphala powder in 2 cups water.
  2. Steep overnight covered, OR simmer 10 min then strain.
  3. Drink 100–150 ml warm in morning on empty stomach.

Eye wash (traditional)

  1. Prepare decoction above.
  2. Strain twice through fine muslin, then through coffee filter.
  3. Cool to body temp. Use in an eye cup, 30 seconds per eye.
  4. Only use freshly made, sterile decoction. Discard after one use.

Traditional benefits

  • Gentle daily bowel regulation (not habit-forming).
  • Gut-mucosa repair, microbiome balance.
  • Eye health (internal + eyewash).
  • Supports liver and cholesterol.
  • Antioxidant / rasayana (rejuvenative) for long-term use.

Cautions

  • Pregnancy: avoid regular internal use — haritaki can be too strong.
  • Breastfeeding: use only under practitioner guidance.
  • Diarrhea or dehydration: discontinue.
  • Blood thinners, diabetes meds, thyroid meds: consult doctor — may potentiate.
  • Children: smaller dose (¼ tsp) only under guidance.
  • Allergy: discontinue if rash / GI distress.

Quality tips

  • Buy organic, seedless, single-origin fruits if making at home.
  • Commercial powders vary — look for: organic certification, no fillers, country of origin stated, ideally batch-tested for heavy metals (ash, lead, mercury).
  • Trusted brands: Banyan Botanicals, Organic India, Kottakkal, Baidyanath, Pukka.

Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) — Safety, Side Effects & Interactions

Ashwagandha is a well-studied adaptogen used for stress, sleep, strength, and fertility. While generally well-tolerated short-term, it has meaningful contraindications.

Typical dosage (adult)

  • Root powder: 1–6 g/day.
  • Standardized extract (e.g., KSM-66, Sensoril): 300–600 mg/day, divided.
  • Decoction / milk decoction (kshira paka): ½–1 tsp root powder simmered 10 min in milk, once daily.
  • Duration: Most clinical trials run 8–12 weeks. Traditionally used longer, but periodic breaks (e.g., 6 weeks on / 2 off) are prudent.

Common side effects (usually mild)

  • Upset stomach, nausea, loose stools (especially on empty stomach — take with food).
  • Drowsiness (usually desired; take at night if so).
  • Headache in first few days.
  • Increased body heat / "warming" feeling (pitta aggravation) — worse in hot climates or pitta constitution.

Less common

  • Skin rash / allergy.
  • Mild liver enzyme elevations — rare case reports of drug-induced liver injury (DILI) exist; discontinue if dark urine, jaundice, nausea, fatigue appear.
  • Worsening acid reflux.
  • Vivid dreams.

Contraindications — avoid or use only with supervision

  • Pregnancy: traditionally abortifacient in large doses; avoid.
  • Breastfeeding: insufficient safety data; avoid.
  • Autoimmune disease (Hashimoto’s, Graves’, lupus, MS, RA): may stimulate immune activity — use only with specialist guidance.
  • Hyperthyroidism: ashwagandha raises T3/T4 in many people; can worsen hyperthyroid symptoms. Conversely it can help hypothyroid — but still monitor labs.
  • Active liver disease: avoid given DILI reports.
  • Prostate cancer (hormone-sensitive): conflicting evidence; avoid without oncologist input.
  • Scheduled surgery: stop ≥ 2 weeks before (sedative + possible bleeding effects).
  • Nightshade allergy: Solanaceae family — use caution.
  • Children: use only under practitioner guidance.

Drug interactions

  • Sedatives (benzodiazepines, Z-drugs, barbiturates, alcohol): additive CNS depression.
  • Thyroid medication (levothyroxine): may increase thyroid hormones — monitor TSH/T4.
  • Immunosuppressants (after transplant, methotrexate, biologics): may antagonize.
  • Diabetes medications: may lower blood sugar further.
  • Antihypertensives: may lower blood pressure further.
  • Hepatotoxic drugs (acetaminophen high-dose, statins, antifungals): theoretical added liver stress.
  • MAOIs / SSRIs: limited data; introduce cautiously.
  • Testosterone-modulating therapy: ashwagandha may raise testosterone/DHEA-S.

Quality & sourcing guidance

  • Prefer root (not leaf) products — most classical use.
  • Look for standardized extracts (KSM-66 or Sensoril) with a Certificate of Analysis for:
    • Withanolide content (2.5–5% for KSM-66).
    • Heavy metals (lead, mercury, arsenic, cadmium).
    • Pesticides and microbial limits.
  • Trusted brands: Himalaya, Organic India, Banyan Botanicals, Pukka, Ixoreal KSM-66, Jarrow.
  • Avoid unbranded bulk churna of unknown origin — Ayurvedic heavy-metal contamination has been documented in some uncertified products.

Signs to stop and consult a doctor

  • Jaundice, dark urine, right-upper-quadrant pain.
  • Persistent nausea, vomiting, severe diarrhea.
  • Palpitations, tremor, heat intolerance (possible thyroid over-stimulation).
  • Rash, swelling, breathing difficulty (allergy).
  • Worsening mood or sleep after 2+ weeks.

Summary

Short-term (up to ~3 months), moderate-dose ashwagandha is well-tolerated for most healthy adults. It is not appropriate in pregnancy, uncontrolled hyperthyroidism, autoimmune flare, active liver disease, or alongside certain medications without medical supervision.
