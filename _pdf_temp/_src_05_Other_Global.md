# 50 Other Global Ancient & Herbal Remedies

Covers Europe, Middle East, Africa, North America, Pacific, and Southeast Asia.

## Europe & Mediterranean

### 1. Echinacea (*Echinacea purpurea*) — Native American / Europe
- **Benefit:** Immune, colds, wound healing.
- **Recipe:** Tincture 2–4 ml 3×/day at onset of cold; max 10 days.
- **Sourcing:** Health stores US/Europe; A. Vogel Echinaforce common.

### 2. Elderberry / Sambucus (*Sambucus nigra*)
- **Benefit:** Antiviral, flu severity reduction.
- **Recipe:** Syrup — 500 g berries + 1 L water simmer 30 min, strain, add equal honey. 1 tbsp daily.
- **Sourcing:** Wild hedges; health stores.

### 3. Garlic (*Allium sativum*) — Mediterranean/Egyptian
- **Benefit:** Antimicrobial, BP, cholesterol.
- **Recipe:** Crush 1–2 cloves, rest 10 min, swallow with honey or in food.
- **Sourcing:** Any grocer.

### 4. Oregano (*Origanum vulgare*)
- **Benefit:** Antimicrobial, respiratory.
- **Recipe:** Steam inhalation with oregano; oil 1 drop in capsule with olive oil.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets, herbolarios.

### 5. Thyme (*Thymus vulgaris*)
- **Benefit:** Cough, bronchitis, antibacterial.
- **Recipe:** Infusion 1 tsp + honey + lemon 3×/day.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

### 6. Rosemary (*Salvia rosmarinus*)
- **Benefit:** Memory, circulation, hair.
- **Recipe:** Infusion: 1 tsp dried leaf in 240 ml hot water, 8–10 min; 1–2 cups/day. Vinegar hair rinse: 2 tbsp dried in 500 ml apple-cider vinegar 2 weeks; dilute 1:4 with water before use.
- **Sourcing:** Mediterranean gardens; supermarkets.

### 7. Sage (*Salvia officinalis*)
- **Benefit:** Sore throat, sweating, menopause.
- **Recipe:** Gargle: 1 tsp dried sage in 240 ml hot water, cooled 10 min; gargle 30 sec 3–4× daily for sore throat. Hot flashes: 1 cup tea 1–2× daily, limit to 4 weeks.
- **Sourcing:** Herb shops.

### 8. Lavender (*Lavandula angustifolia*)
- **Benefit:** Anxiety, sleep, burns.
- **Recipe:** Sachet under pillow; essential oil diluted 2% in carrier.
- **Sourcing:** Provence; everywhere.

### 9. Chamomile (*Matricaria chamomilla*)
- **Benefit:** Sleep, colic, digestion, skin.
- **Recipe:** Infusion 1 tsp 5 min; cooled compress for eyes.
- **Sourcing:** Every pharmacy.

### 10. Peppermint (*Mentha × piperita*)
- **Benefit:** IBS, nausea, headache.
- **Recipe:** IBS: 1 enteric-coated capsule (≈0.2 ml peppermint oil) 3× daily between meals, 4–12 weeks. Leaf tea: 1 tsp dried leaf in 240 ml hot water 8 min, 1–3 cups/day.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

### 11. Fennel (*Foeniculum vulgare*)
- **Benefit:** Colic, bloating, lactation.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp crushed seeds in hot water 10 min.
- **Sourcing:** Spice shops.

### 12. Calendula (*Calendula officinalis*)
- **Benefit:** Wounds, eczema, skin.
- **Recipe:** Oil infusion: 1 cup dried flowers in 500 ml olive oil, 4–6 weeks low heat / windowsill; strain. Salve: 100 ml infused oil + 15 g beeswax melted together, poured into jars. Apply 2–3× daily to cuts, rashes, nappy-rash.
- **Sourcing:** Herb shops; Weleda creams.

### 13. St John’s Wort (*Hypericum perforatum*)
- **Benefit:** Mild depression; nerve pain (topical).
- **Recipe:** Red oil — flowers in olive oil, sun-macerated 4 weeks. Internal tincture under care.
- **Caution:** Interacts with many drugs (contraceptives, SSRIs, warfarin, HIV meds).
- **Sourcing:** Herb shops.

### 14. Valerian (*Valeriana officinalis*)
- **Benefit:** Sleep, anxiety.
- **Recipe:** Root decoction 1 hr before bed; capsules 400–600 mg.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies.

### 15. Hops (*Humulus lupulus*)
- **Benefit:** Sleep, anxiety, menopause.
- **Recipe:** Sleep pillow: 50 g dried hop strobiles in a cotton pouch, placed near the head. Tea: 1 tsp dried strobile in 240 ml hot water, 8 min; 1 cup 30 min before bed.
- **Sourcing:** Brewing + herb shops.

### 16. Milk thistle (*Silybum marianum*)
- **Benefit:** Liver protection, hepatitis supportive.
- **Recipe:** Crushed seeds 1 tsp in smoothie; standardized silymarin capsules.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies.

### 17. Dandelion (*Taraxacum officinale*)
- **Benefit:** Liver, diuretic, digestion.
- **Recipe:** Root decoction: 1 tsp dried root in 240 ml water simmered 15 min; 1 cup 1–2× daily. Salad: 1 handful young leaves. Roasted root “coffee”: 1 tsp roasted ground root in 240 ml hot water, 5 min steep.
- **Sourcing:** Wild; herb shops.

### 18. Nettle (*Urtica dioica*)
- **Benefit:** Allergies, iron, joints.
- **Recipe:** Food: 200 g fresh young nettle tops blanched 1 min, used like spinach. Tea: 1 tbsp dried leaf in 240 ml hot water 10 min; 1–3 cups/day.
- **Sourcing:** Wild (gloves); herb shops.

### 19. Yarrow (*Achillea millefolium*)
- **Benefit:** Wounds, fever (diaphoretic), menstrual.
- **Recipe:** Leaf poultice for cuts; infusion 1 tsp for fever.
- **Sourcing:** Wild; herb shops.

### 20. Linden flower / Tilia
- **Benefit:** Fever, anxiety, sleep.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp flowers infused 10 min.
- **Sourcing:** European pharmacies.

### 21. Hawthorn (*Crataegus*) — European
- **Benefit:** Mild heart failure, BP, anxiety.
- **Recipe:** Standardized extract 300–900 mg/day; flower + leaf tea.
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies.

### 22. Ginkgo (*Ginkgo biloba*) — China/Europe
- **Benefit:** Circulation, cognitive support.
- **Recipe:** Standardized extract 120–240 mg/day (not raw leaves).
- **Sourcing:** Pharmacies.

### 23. Bilberry (*Vaccinium myrtillus*)
- **Benefit:** Night vision, capillaries.
- **Recipe:** Fresh: 1 handful (≈80 g) berries daily. Standardized extract: 80–160 mg (25% anthocyanins) 2× daily.
- **Sourcing:** Nordic/European markets.

### 24. Mullein (*Verbascum thapsus*)
- **Benefit:** Cough, bronchitis, ear oil.
- **Recipe:** Leaf tea: 1 tsp dried leaf in 240 ml hot water 10 min; strain through fine muslin to remove hairs; 1–3 cups/day. Ear oil: 2–3 drops warm flower-infused oil in the ear canal — intact eardrum only.
- **Sourcing:** Wild; herb shops.

## Middle East & North Africa

### 25. Black seed / Habbat al-Barakah (*Nigella sativa*)
- **Benefit:** Immune, respiratory, blood sugar.
- **Recipe:** ½ tsp seeds or 1 tsp oil with honey daily.
- **Sourcing:** Middle Eastern groceries; spice souks.

### 26. Frankincense / Luban (*Boswellia sacra / serrata*)
- **Benefit:** Joint inflammation, asthma; resin chewed for oral health.
- **Recipe:** Resin water: 2–3 pea-sized pieces (≈2 g) in 240 ml water overnight; drink on empty stomach. Standardized extract: 300–400 mg *Boswellia serrata* (60% boswellic acids) 2–3× daily with meals.
- **Sourcing:** Oman, Yemen souks; spice shops.

### 27. Myrrh (*Commiphora myrrha*)
- **Benefit:** Gum health, antimicrobial.
- **Recipe:** Mouth rinse: 1 ml myrrh tincture in 30 ml water; swish 30 sec, spit; 2–3× daily for gum inflammation. Short-term only.
- **Sourcing:** Middle Eastern souks.

### 28. Henna (*Lawsonia inermis*)
- **Benefit:** Hair/skin dye, cooling, antifungal.
- **Recipe:** 100 g henna powder + 100 ml lemon juice or plain yogurt, rested 6–12 hr; apply to hair 2–4 hr then rinse. Topical only — never ingested; patch-test first.
- **Sourcing:** Everywhere in MENA/South Asia.

### 29. Date + milk (traditional)
- **Benefit:** Anemia, energy, pregnancy support.
- **Recipe:** 3 dates soaked in warm milk overnight.
- **Sourcing:** Markets throughout MENA.

### 30. Za’atar blend (thyme + sumac + sesame)
- **Benefit:** Digestive, antimicrobial, antioxidant.
- **Recipe:** 1 tbsp with olive oil as bread dip.
- **Sourcing:** Middle Eastern groceries.

## Sub-Saharan Africa

### 32. Honeybush (*Cyclopia*)

### 33. African potato (*Hypoxis hemerocallidea*)

### 34. Sutherlandia / Cancer bush (*Lessertia frutescens*)

- **Sourcing:** SA herb shops.

### 35. Moringa (*Moringa oleifera*) — Africa/India

- **Sourcing:** Markets across Africa.

### 36. Baobab fruit (*Adansonia digitata*)
- **Benefit:** Vitamin C, fiber, gut.

- **Sourcing:** West/Southern African markets.

### 37. Shea butter (*Vitellaria paradoxa*)
- **Benefit:** Skin, burns, eczema.

- **Sourcing:** West African markets.

### 38. Hibiscus / Bissap / Karkadé (*Hibiscus sabdariffa*)
- **Benefit:** Blood pressure, vitamin C.

- **Sourcing:** Sudan, Egypt, West Africa; markets globally.

## Native American / North America

### 39. Sage smudge / White sage (*Salvia apiana*)
- **Benefit:** Ceremonial cleansing; traditional respiratory use.
- **Recipe:** Small bundle burned briefly (5–10 sec); smoke wafted through space. Use ethically-harvested sources only — white sage is culturally sensitive; non-Indigenous users may prefer rosemary, garden sage, or cedar.
- **Sourcing:** Buy from Indigenous sources; wild populations are threatened.

### 40. Sweetgrass / Cedar / Tobacco — Four Sacred Medicines
- **Benefit:** Ceremonial across many First Nations.
- **Preparation:** Traditional protocols — learn from cultural knowledge keepers.
- **Sourcing:** First Nations cultural centers.

### 41. Slippery elm (*Ulmus rubra*)
- **Benefit:** Sore throat, GERD, gut lining.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp inner-bark powder in warm water → mucilage.
- **Sourcing:** Health stores.

### 42. Goldenseal (*Hydrastis canadensis*)
- **Benefit:** Mucous membranes, antimicrobial; threatened — use cultivated.
- **Recipe:** Tincture 1–3 ml 3×/day short term.
- **Sourcing:** Herb stores (certified cultivated).

## Pacific & Oceania

- **Benefit:** Anxiety, sleep, social relaxation.

- **Caution:** Avoid alcohol and hepatotoxic drugs; choose noble cultivars.
- **Sourcing:** Fiji, Vanuatu, Tonga markets; reputable online vendors.

### 44. Manuka honey (*Leptospermum scoparium*) — NZ
- **Benefit:** Wound healing, throat, antimicrobial (UMF rated).
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp on toast; topical on wounds.
- **Sourcing:** NZ supermarkets; look for UMF 10+ for therapeutic use.

### 45. Kawakawa (*Piper excelsum*) — Māori
- **Benefit:** Digestion, skin, circulation.
- **Recipe:** 3–4 young leaves with holes (insect-bitten) in hot water 10 min.
- **Sourcing:** NZ herbal shops; wild on North Island.

### 50. Betel leaf (*Piper betle*) — South/SE Asia

### 51. Cleavers (*Galium aparine*) — Global weed

### 52. Chickweed (*Stellaria media*) — Global weed

### 53. Fig (*Ficus carica*) — Mediterranean

### 54. Rosehip (*Rosa canina*) — Global

### 55. Turkey Tail (*Trametes versicolor*) — Global mushroom
