# 50 Peruvian / Andean / Spanish Folk Remedies

> Sourcing hubs: **Mercado San Pedro (Cusco)**, **Mercado Belén (Iquitos)**, **Mercado de Surquillo / Mercado Central (Lima)**, **Mercado Modelo (Chiclayo)**, Andean ferias in Puno/Huancayo. Spanish folk herbs from *herbolarios* (herb shops) across Spain and Latin America.

## 1. Coca leaf (*Erythroxylum coca*)
- **Benefit:** Altitude sickness (soroche), energy, appetite, digestion.
- **Recipe:** *Mate de coca* — 1 tbsp leaves in hot water 5 min.
- **Sourcing:** Legal in Peru/Bolivia; markets of Cusco, Puno, La Paz. Illegal in most other countries.

## 2. Maca (*Lepidium meyenii*)
- **Benefit:** Stamina, hormonal balance, libido, fertility.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp gelatinized powder in smoothie/oats.
- **Sourcing:** Junín plateau; Lima health stores, supermarkets.

## 3. Cat’s claw / Uña de gato (*Uncaria tomentosa*)
- **Benefit:** Immune, anti-inflammatory, arthritis.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp inner-bark shreds simmered 15 min; 1–2 cups daily.
- **Sourcing:** Amazonian markets; *herbolarios* in Lima.

## 4. Sangre de grado / Sangre de drago (*Croton lechleri*)
- **Benefit:** Wound healing, cold sores, topical antimicrobial.
- **Recipe:** 2–3 drops red latex rubbed onto clean wound until it turns pale.
- **Sourcing:** Amazonian pharmacies (Iquitos, Pucallpa).

## 5. Muña (*Minthostachys mollis* — Andean mint)
- **Benefit:** Indigestion, altitude bloating, cold.
- **Recipe:** Infusion — 1 tsp dried leaves in hot water 5 min post-meal.
- **Sourcing:** Andean markets; herbolarios.

## 6. Chuchuhuasi (*Maytenus macrocarpa*)
- **Benefit:** Joint pain, muscle ache, energy tonic.
- **Recipe:** *Aguardiente* maceration — bark in cane alcohol 2–4 weeks; 1 shot glass dose.
- **Sourcing:** Amazonian markets.

## 7. Copaiba oil (*Copaifera* spp.)
- **Benefit:** Anti-inflammatory, skin, wound, gums.
- **Recipe:** 1–2 drops in carrier oil topically; internally only under guidance.
- **Sourcing:** Iquitos markets; naturistas.

## 8. Aguaymanto / Goldenberry (*Physalis peruviana*)
- **Benefit:** Vitamin C, antioxidant, blood sugar.
- **Recipe:** Fresh: 10–15 berries daily. Dried as snack: 1–2 tbsp. Juice: 120 ml once daily.
- **Sourcing:** Markets in Cajamarca, Ancash; supermarkets.

## 9. Camu camu (*Myrciaria dubia*)
- **Benefit:** Highest-known natural vitamin C, antiviral.
- **Recipe:** ½ tsp powder in water/juice; fresh pulp juice in Amazon.
- **Sourcing:** Loreto markets; Lima health stores.

## 10. Sacha inchi (*Plukenetia volubilis*)
- **Benefit:** Omega-3/6, cholesterol, brain.
- **Recipe:** Toasted seeds as snack; 1 tbsp oil on salads.
- **Sourcing:** San Martín/Ucayali markets; Lima supermarkets.

## 11. Hercampuri (*Gentianella alborosea*)
- **Benefit:** Liver, cholesterol, weight.
- **Recipe:** Decoction — 1 tsp in 1 cup water 10 min; bitter.
- **Sourcing:** Andean herbolarios.

## 12. Chancapiedra / Stonebreaker (*Phyllanthus niruri*)
- **Benefit:** Kidney stones, gallstones, liver.
- **Recipe:** 1 tbsp dried herb in 2 cups water, boil 10 min; 2 cups/day.
- **Sourcing:** Amazonian and Andean markets.

## 13. Boldo (*Peumus boldus*)
- **Benefit:** Liver, gallbladder, digestion.
- **Recipe:** Infusion — 1 tsp leaves in hot water 5 min after fatty meals.
- **Sourcing:** Chilean/Peruvian herbolarios; Spain.

## 14. Hierba Luisa / Lemon verbena (*Aloysia citrodora*)
- **Benefit:** Digestion, anxiety, sleep.
- **Recipe:** Infusion — 5–6 fresh leaves in hot water.
- **Sourcing:** Home gardens; markets across Peru & Spain.

## 15. Manayupa / Desmodium (*Desmodium molliculum*)
- **Benefit:** Allergies, liver, blood purifier.
- **Recipe:** 1 tbsp dried herb simmered 5 min; 2 cups/day.
- **Sourcing:** Herbolarios in Peru.

## 16. Paico / Epazote (*Dysphania ambrosioides*)
- **Benefit:** Antiparasitic, gas, digestion.
- **Recipe:** Infusion — 1 tsp fresh leaves; short cures, not in pregnancy.
- **Sourcing:** Markets across Peru, Mexico.

## 17. Huamanripa (*Senecio tephrosioides*)
- **Benefit:** Bronchitis, cough, altitude respiratory issues.
- **Recipe:** Decoction — 1 tbsp in water + honey.
- **Sourcing:** Andean ferias.

## 18. Matico (*Piper aduncum*)
- **Benefit:** Wound healing, ulcers, antimicrobial.
- **Recipe:** Wounds: 3–4 fresh leaves crushed, applied 15–20 min, 2–3× daily. Gastritis: 1 tbsp dried leaves in 240 ml just-off-boil water, 10 min steep, 2× daily before meals.
- **Sourcing:** Amazonian and Andean markets.

## 19. Ayahuasca (*Banisteriopsis caapi* + *Psychotria viridis*)
- **Benefit:** Ceremonial / psychospiritual (traditional use only).
- **Preparation:** Only by trained *curanderos* in ceremonial context.
- **Sourcing:** Traditional Amazonian centers; legality varies internationally.

## 20. San Pedro / Huachuma (*Echinopsis pachanoi*)
- **Benefit:** Ceremonial use in Andean tradition.
- **Preparation:** Traditional curandero decoction only.
- **Sourcing:** Andean curanderos; cactus widely cultivated.

## 21. Yerba mate (*Ilex paraguariensis*) — South American
- **Benefit:** Stimulant, antioxidant, focus.
- **Recipe:** Gourd + bombilla; loose leaves + hot (not boiling) water.
- **Sourcing:** Ubiquitous in Argentina, Paraguay, Uruguay, S. Brazil; supermarkets.

## 22. Achiote / Annatto (*Bixa orellana*)
- **Benefit:** Skin (burns, sun), antioxidant; culinary.
- **Recipe:** Salve: 1 tbsp seeds warmed in 30 ml coconut/olive oil 5 min, strain; apply to skin. Sunburn: 1 tsp fresh-seed paste on affected area 10–15 min, rinse.
- **Sourcing:** Amazonian markets.

## 23. Graviola / Soursop (*Annona muricata*)
- **Benefit:** Calming, sleep, traditional immune use.
- **Recipe:** Leaf infusion — 3 leaves in hot water 10 min.
- **Sourcing:** Tropical Peru, Caribbean markets.

## 24. Chirimoya (*Annona cherimola*)
- **Benefit:** Digestion, iron, antioxidants (fruit).
- **Recipe:** Fresh: ½–1 whole fruit eaten raw. ⚠️ Seeds are toxic — discard, never chew or swallow.
- **Sourcing:** Andean valleys, Lima markets.

## 25. Lucuma (*Pouteria lucuma*)
- **Benefit:** Low-GI sweetener, beta-carotene, iron.
- **Recipe:** 1 tbsp powder in smoothies/oats.
- **Sourcing:** Peruvian markets; health stores.

## 26. Kiwicha / Amaranth (*Amaranthus caudatus*)
- **Benefit:** Complete protein, calcium, iron.
- **Recipe:** Popped: 2 tbsp grain toasted on dry pan 1–2 min until it pops. Porridge: 3 tbsp grain simmered in 240 ml water or milk 20 min.
- **Sourcing:** Andean markets.

## 27. Quinoa (*Chenopodium quinoa*)
- **Benefit:** Complete protein, minerals.
- **Recipe:** Rinse, simmer 1:2 water 15 min.
- **Sourcing:** Universally available.

## 28. Cañihua (*Chenopodium pallidicaule*)
- **Benefit:** Protein, iron, easy digest.
- **Recipe:** 1–2 tbsp toasted flour (*cañihuaco*) stirred into 240 ml warm water, milk, or juice, 1–2× daily.
- **Sourcing:** Puno markets.

## 29. Mashua (*Tropaeolum tuberosum*)
- **Benefit:** Prostate and urinary folk use.
- **Recipe:** Boiled tuber: 1 medium tuber (≈ 100 g) boiled 20 min, eaten as a side. Juice: 100 ml fresh, once daily, short term only (cooling effect on prostate / androgen-sensitive users).
- **Sourcing:** Andean ferias.

## 30. Maca negra (black maca)
- **Benefit:** Cognition, male fertility (stronger than yellow).
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp powder in smoothies.
- **Sourcing:** Junín; specialty health stores.

## 31. Suma / Brazilian ginseng (*Pfaffia paniculata*)
- **Benefit:** Adaptogen, stamina.
- **Recipe:** ½ tsp powder in water.
- **Sourcing:** Amazonian naturistas.

## 32. Jergón sacha (*Dracontium loretense*)
- **Benefit:** Traditional: snake bite, immune support.
- **Recipe:** Tincture or decoction used only under trained curandero / ethnobotanist guidance — typical ritual doses 5–10 ml tincture or a small cup of decoction. Do not self-dose.
- **Sourcing:** Iquitos markets.

## 33. Ajo sacha / "Wild garlic" (*Mansoa alliacea*)
- **Benefit:** Joint pain, colds, fever.
- **Recipe:** Decoction: 1 tbsp dried leaf/bark in 500 ml water, simmered 20 min; 1 cup 2× daily up to 10 days. Ritual bath: 2 handfuls in 4 L hot water, poured over body.
- **Sourcing:** Amazonian markets.

## 34. Chancapiedra syrup (commercial)
- **Benefit:** Kidney & gallbladder support.
- **Recipe:** 1 tbsp 2×/day for short cures.
- **Sourcing:** Peruvian pharmacies.

## 35. Algarrobina (*Prosopis pallida* carob syrup)
- **Benefit:** Iron, energy, traditional tonic.
- **Recipe:** 1 tbsp in milk; also in cocktails.
- **Sourcing:** Piura markets; supermarkets nationally.

## 36. Uña de gavilán (*Uncaria guianensis*)
- **Benefit:** Similar to cat’s claw, joints and immunity.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp inner-bark shreds in 240 ml water, simmered 15–20 min; 1–2 cups daily. Limit to 4–6 weeks at a time.
- **Sourcing:** Amazonian markets.

## 37. Uchu sanango (*Tabernaemontana sananho*)
- **Benefit:** Traditional nerve and strength tonic.
- **Recipe:** Strictly ceremonial / medicinal under trained curandero supervision. Potent psychoactive; not for self-administration.
- **Sourcing:** Amazonian master plant traditions.

## 38. Manzanilla / Chamomile (*Matricaria chamomilla*) — Spanish folk
- **Benefit:** Digestion, sleep, colic.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp flowers in hot water 5 min.
- **Sourcing:** Every herbolario in Spain & Peru.

## 39. Romero / Rosemary (*Salvia rosmarinus*) — Spanish folk
- **Benefit:** Circulation, memory, hair, digestion.
- **Recipe:** Infusion: 1 tsp dried leaf in 240 ml hot water, 8–10 min; 1–2 cups/day. Hair rinse: 2 tbsp dried in 500 ml water simmered 10 min, cooled, poured over clean hair. Joint rub: 2–3 ml tincture diluted in carrier oil, massaged into area 2× daily.
- **Sourcing:** Spanish herbolarios; Mediterranean gardens.

## 40. Tomillo / Thyme (*Thymus vulgaris*) — Spanish folk
- **Benefit:** Cough, throat, antimicrobial.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp dried in hot water + honey + lemon.
- **Sourcing:** Herbolarios and supermarkets.

## 41. Salvia / Sage (*Salvia officinalis*) — Spanish folk
- **Benefit:** Sore throat, hot flashes, oral hygiene.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp dried sage in 240 ml just-off-boil water, covered 10 min, cooled. Gargle 30 sec, 3–4× daily for sore throat. Avoid repeated swallowing in pregnancy.
- **Sourcing:** Herbolarios.

## 42. Malva / Mallow (*Malva sylvestris*) — Spanish folk
- **Benefit:** Cough, constipation, skin.
- **Recipe:** Infusion flowers/leaves 10 min.
- **Sourcing:** Herbolarios.

## 43. Cola de caballo / Horsetail (*Equisetum arvense*) — Spanish/Peruvian
- **Benefit:** Diuretic, nails, hair, bones (silica).
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp dried in water simmered 10 min.
- **Sourcing:** Herbolarios across Hispanic world.

## 44. Llantén / Plantain (*Plantago major*)
- **Benefit:** Wounds, cough, insect bites.
- **Recipe:** Bites/stings: crush 2–3 fresh leaves, apply 15–20 min. Cough syrup: 2 handfuls fresh leaves + 300 ml water simmered 20 min, strain, add 200 g honey; 1 tbsp 3× daily.
- **Sourcing:** Wild weed; herbolarios.

## 45. Ortiga / Nettle (*Urtica dioica*)
- **Benefit:** Allergies, anemia, joints, prostate.
- **Recipe:** Cooked like spinach; tea of dried leaves.
- **Sourcing:** Wild; herbolarios.

## 46. Diente de león / Dandelion (*Taraxacum officinale*)
- **Benefit:** Liver, diuretic, digestion.
- **Recipe:** Leaves: 1 handful young leaves raw in salad. Root decoction: 1 tsp dried root in 240 ml water, simmered 15 min; 1 cup 1–2× daily as a gentle liver tonic.
- **Sourcing:** Wild; herbolarios.

## 47. Aloe / Sábila (*Aloe vera*) — Spanish
- **Benefit:** Burns, skin, constipation.
- **Recipe:** Topical: ½ tsp fresh inner gel on burns/irritation, 1–3× daily. Smoothie: 1–2 tbsp filleted inner gel (skin and yellow latex removed) in 240 ml juice/water — short-term only, not during pregnancy.
- **Sourcing:** Home plants; herbolarios.

## 48. Orégano peruano (*Lippia graveolens*)
- **Benefit:** Menstrual cramps, digestion.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp in hot water; 2 cups/day during cramps.
- **Sourcing:** Tacna and Arequipa markets.

## 49. Valeriana (*Valeriana officinalis*)
- **Benefit:** Sleep, anxiety.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp root simmered 10 min at night.
- **Sourcing:** Herbolarios in Spain and Peru.

## 50. Pasiflora / Passionflower (*Passiflora incarnata*)
- **Benefit:** Anxiety, insomnia.
- **Recipe:** 1 tsp dried aerial parts infused 10 min.
- **Sourcing:** Herbolarios across Hispanic world.
