# 50 Chinese (TCM) Herbal Remedies

> Sourcing hubs: **Beijing Tongrentang (同仁堂)** branches nationwide, **Anguo herbal market (Hebei)**, **Bozhou herb market (Anhui)** — the largest in China, **Lei Yun Shang (Shanghai)**, local *zhongyao* pharmacies, hospital TCM dispensaries. Overseas: Chinatowns worldwide. Pinyin (and characters) given for accurate shop requests.

> Caution: TCM herbs are typically prescribed in formulas by a trained TCM doctor based on syndrome differentiation. Self-use should be short-term, low-dose, and avoid contraindications (pregnancy, medication interactions).

## 1. Ginseng / Ren Shen 人参 (*Panax ginseng*)
- **Benefit:** Qi tonic — fatigue, shock, lung/spleen qi.
- **Recipe:** 3–5 g sliced root, double-boiled 1–2 hr; or chew single slice.
- **Sourcing:** Jilin Changbai mountain; Tongrentang.

## 2. Astragalus / Huang Qi 黄芪 (*Astragalus membranaceus*)
- **Benefit:** Immune, wei qi, chronic fatigue, wound healing.
- **Recipe:** 10–15 g slices simmered 30 min; add to chicken soup.
- **Sourcing:** Gansu, Inner Mongolia; all TCM pharmacies.

## 3. Goji / Gou Qi Zi 枸杞子 (*Lycium barbarum*)
- **Benefit:** Liver-kidney yin, eyes, blood.
- **Recipe:** 15–20 berries in hot water or in congee.
- **Sourcing:** Ningxia is premium; supermarkets.

## 4. Fresh ginger / Sheng Jiang 生姜
- **Benefit:** Warms stomach, wind-cold, nausea.
- **Recipe:** 3 slices + brown sugar + hong zao, simmered 10 min.
- **Sourcing:** Any market.

## 5. Licorice / Gan Cao 甘草 (*Glycyrrhiza uralensis*)
- **Benefit:** Harmonizes formulas, soothes throat, spleen qi.
- **Recipe:** 3–6 g sliced in formulas; short cures only.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 6. White peony / Bai Shao 白芍 (*Paeonia lactiflora*)
- **Benefit:** Blood tonic, cramps, liver.
- **Recipe:** 9–15 g in formulas (e.g., Si Wu Tang).
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 7. Dang Gui 当归 (*Angelica sinensis*)
- **Benefit:** Blood tonic, menstruation, circulation.
- **Recipe:** 6–12 g in soups with chicken and huang qi.
- **Sourcing:** Gansu Min Xian is premium.

## 8. Reishi / Ling Zhi 灵芝 (*Ganoderma lucidum*)
- **Benefit:** Shen-calming, immune, liver.
- **Recipe:** 5–10 g dried slices simmered 1 hr.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies; mushroom shops.

## 9. Chrysanthemum / Ju Hua 菊花 (*Chrysanthemum morifolium*)
- **Benefit:** Cools liver heat, eyes, headache.
- **Recipe:** 5–10 flowers in hot water 5 min, often + goji.
- **Sourcing:** Hangzhou (bai ju) famous; supermarkets.

## 10. Hawthorn / Shan Zha 山楂 (*Crataegus pinnatifida*)
- **Benefit:** Digests meat/fat, circulation, cholesterol.
- **Recipe:** 10 g simmered 15 min after heavy meals.
- **Sourcing:** Markets; candied on sticks *tanghulu*.

## 11. Dang Shen 党参 (*Codonopsis pilosula*)
- **Benefit:** Gentle qi tonic, mild ginseng substitute.
- **Recipe:** 9–15 g in soups.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 12. He Shou Wu 何首乌 (*Polygonum multiflorum*) — *prepared* only
- **Benefit:** Hair, liver-kidney yin, graying.
- **Recipe:** Use *zhi he shou wu* (prepared), 6–12 g in decoction.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies; raw form can be hepatotoxic.

## 13. Bai Zhu 白术 (*Atractylodes macrocephala*)
- **Benefit:** Spleen qi, damp, bloating.
- **Recipe:** 6–12 g in Si Jun Zi Tang.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 14. Fu Ling 茯苓 (*Poria cocos*)
- **Benefit:** Drains damp, calms shen, mild diuretic.
- **Recipe:** 9–15 g in soups/formulas.
- **Sourcing:** Yunnan premium; TCM pharmacies.

## 15. Chen Pi 陈皮 (aged tangerine peel)
- **Benefit:** Moves qi, phlegm, digestion.
- **Recipe:** 3–9 g aged peel in tea/soup.
- **Sourcing:** Xinhui Guangdong famous.

## 16. Ban Xia 半夏 (*Pinellia ternata*) — *processed*
- **Benefit:** Phlegm, nausea.
- **Recipe:** Only processed 3–9 g in formulas (raw is toxic).
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 17. Jin Yin Hua 金银花 (*Lonicera japonica*, honeysuckle)
- **Benefit:** Clears heat-toxin, sore throat, early colds.
- **Recipe:** 6–15 g dried flowers as tea.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies, supermarkets.

## 18. Lian Qiao 连翘 (*Forsythia suspensa*)
- **Benefit:** Heat-toxin, often paired with Jin Yin Hua.
- **Recipe:** In Yin Qiao San formula. Standalone: 6–15 g dried fruit in 400 ml water, simmered 15 min; 1–2 cups/day short-term for early-stage sore throat / cold.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 19. Ban Lan Gen 板蓝根 (*Isatis*)
- **Benefit:** Antiviral, sore throat, flu prevention.
- **Recipe:** Granule sachets 1 sachet in hot water.
- **Sourcing:** All Chinese pharmacies.

## 20. Huang Lian 黄连 (*Coptis chinensis*)
- **Benefit:** Damp-heat, diarrhea, mouth ulcers.
- **Recipe:** 2–5 g in formulas; very bitter.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 21. Huang Qin 黄芩 (*Scutellaria baicalensis*)
- **Benefit:** Lung/upper heat, allergies.
- **Recipe:** 6–12 g in formulas.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 22. Huang Bai 黄柏 (*Phellodendron amurense*)
- **Benefit:** Lower-jiao damp-heat, UTIs.
- **Recipe:** 6–12 g in formulas.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 23. Bo He 薄荷 (Chinese mint, *Mentha haplocalyx*)
- **Benefit:** Wind-heat cold, headache, eyes.
- **Recipe:** 3–6 g added at end of decoction.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets, TCM shops.

## 24. Ju Hong / Hong Pao 红茶 — pu-erh tea (*Camellia sinensis*)
- **Benefit:** Digestion of fats, cholesterol.
- **Recipe:** Rinse, then steep 2–5 min multiple infusions.
- **Sourcing:** Yunnan; tea shops nationwide.

## 25. Dried longan / Long Yan Rou 龙眼肉
- **Benefit:** Blood & heart-spleen tonic, insomnia.
- **Recipe:** 10 g in soup with hong zao + goji.
- **Sourcing:** Markets.

## 26. Hong Zao / Red dates 红枣 (*Ziziphus jujuba*)
- **Benefit:** Qi-blood tonic, calms shen.
- **Recipe:** 5–10 dates in tea/soup.
- **Sourcing:** Every market; Xinjiang premium.

## 27. Suan Zao Ren 酸枣仁 (sour jujube seed)
- **Benefit:** Insomnia, anxiety.
- **Recipe:** 9–18 g crushed, simmered.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 28. Yuan Zhi 远志 (*Polygala*)
- **Benefit:** Shen-calming, memory.
- **Recipe:** 3–9 g in formulas.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 29. Bai He 百合 (lily bulb)
- **Benefit:** Lung yin, dry cough, anxiety.
- **Recipe:** 9–30 g in sweet soup with pear.
- **Sourcing:** Markets; Gansu Lanzhou premium.

## 30. Sha Shen 沙参 (*Glehnia* / *Adenophora*)
- **Benefit:** Lung/stomach yin, dry cough.
- **Recipe:** 9–15 g in soups.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 31. Mai Men Dong 麦门冬 (*Ophiopogon*)
- **Benefit:** Yin tonic, dry mouth, cough.
- **Recipe:** 6–12 g in tea.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 32. Shan Yao 山药 (Chinese yam, *Dioscorea opposita*)
- **Benefit:** Spleen-lung-kidney tonic, diabetes.
- **Recipe:** Fresh in soups; dried 15–30 g in formulas.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets (fresh); TCM shops (dried).

## 33. Yi Yi Ren 薏苡仁 (Job’s tears / Coix)
- **Benefit:** Drains damp, skin, edema.
- **Recipe:** Soak, simmer 1 hr in congee with red beans.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 34. Chi Xiao Dou 赤小豆 (adzuki)
- **Benefit:** Damp-drain, edema.
- **Recipe:** Congee: 30 g adzuki + 30 g yi yi ren (Job’s tears) + 100 g rice + 1.5 L water, simmered 1 hr. Eat warm, 1 bowl daily for damp-heat / edema.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 35. Lian Zi 莲子 (lotus seed)
- **Benefit:** Spleen, heart, sleep.
- **Recipe:** 15 g in sweet soup or congee.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 36. Bai Mu Er 白木耳 (white fungus / tremella)
- **Benefit:** Lung yin, skin, anti-aging.
- **Recipe:** 15 g dried tremella soaked 1 hr, simmered 45 min with 6 red dates + 10 g rock sugar in 800 ml water. Serves 2. Moistens lungs and skin.
- **Sourcing:** Fujian; markets.

## 37. Hei Mu Er 黑木耳 (black wood ear)
- **Benefit:** Blood thinner, circulation.
- **Recipe:** 10 g dried wood ear soaked 30 min in warm water; stir-fry with vegetables, or cold salad with 1 tbsp rice vinegar + 1 tsp sesame oil. 1 serving 2–3×/week.
- **Sourcing:** Supermarkets.

## 38. Gui Zhi 桂枝 (cinnamon twig)
- **Benefit:** Releases exterior cold, circulation.
- **Recipe:** 3–9 g in Gui Zhi Tang decoction.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 39. Rou Gui 肉桂 (cinnamon bark)
- **Benefit:** Warms kidney yang, cold limbs.
- **Recipe:** 1–5 g added late in decoction.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies; Guangxi.

## 40. Da Huang 大黄 (rhubarb root)
- **Benefit:** Strong laxative, heat-clearing (short-term).
- **Recipe:** 3–9 g in formulas under guidance.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 41. Ma Huang 麻黄 (*Ephedra*)
- **Benefit:** Wind-cold with wheezing; strong.
- **Recipe:** Prescription only — 3–9 g in classical decoction (e.g., Ma Huang Tang) when dispensed by a TCM physician. Restricted / illegal in many jurisdictions. Dangerous with MAOIs, stimulants, cardiac/thyroid medication.
- **Sourcing:** TCM hospitals; restricted in many countries.

## 42. Xing Ren 杏仁 (bitter apricot kernel) — *processed*
- **Benefit:** Cough, wheezing, constipation.
- **Recipe:** Processed kernel 3–9 g in decoction.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 43. Pi Pa Ye 枇杷叶 (loquat leaf)
- **Benefit:** Cough, lung heat.
- **Recipe:** 6–12 g in decoction; commercial *pi pa gao* syrup.
- **Sourcing:** Nin Jiom syrup widely sold.

## 44. San Qi / Tian Qi 三七 (*Panax notoginseng*)
- **Benefit:** Stops bleeding, trauma, circulation.
- **Recipe:** 1–3 g powder in warm water.
- **Sourcing:** Wenshan Yunnan.

## 45. Du Zhong 杜仲 (*Eucommia ulmoides*)
- **Benefit:** Lower back, kidney yang, hypertension.
- **Recipe:** 6–15 g in decoction or soup.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 46. Nu Zhen Zi 女贞子 (*Ligustrum lucidum*)
- **Benefit:** Liver-kidney yin, eyes, hair.
- **Recipe:** 6–15 g in formulas.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 47. Gou Teng 钩藤 (*Uncaria rhynchophylla*)
- **Benefit:** Liver wind, hypertension, headache.
- **Recipe:** 6–12 g added late in decoction.
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies.

## 48. Tian Ma 天麻 (*Gastrodia elata*)
- **Benefit:** Headache, dizziness, spasms.
- **Recipe:** 3–9 g in soup (steamed with fish).
- **Sourcing:** TCM pharmacies; Yunnan.

## 49. Cordyceps / Dong Chong Xia Cao 冬虫夏草
- **Benefit:** Lung-kidney tonic, stamina.
- **Recipe:** 3–9 g double-boiled with duck/chicken.
- **Sourcing:** Tibet/Qinghai; very expensive, many fakes.

## 50. Yunnan Baiyao 云南白药 (proprietary powder)
- **Benefit:** Bleeding, trauma, bruises.
- **Recipe:** Topical: thin layer sprinkled on clean wound, cover. Oral: 1 capsule (0.25 g) 2–3× daily for 1–3 days per package insert; the emergency red pill per acute trauma instructions.
- **Sourcing:** All Chinese pharmacies.
